Path One of Two
By Just Bank AI
"Okay… cute robot. Now what?"

The purpose of Path One is to help someone build their Claude foundation when there are no digital breadcrumbs to pull from yet. They do not have a trained AI workspace. They do not have saved ChatGPT memories. They do not have years of prompt history, corrections, visual rules, or brand preferences sitting inside another AI tool.

So instead of extracting, they are going to build. This path helps Claude interview them, understand their voice, uncover their visual style, organize their brand and content direction, create their first AI Twin rules, and build a project folder that can grow with them.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is to give Claude something real to work with.

What You Are Building

By the time you finish Path One, you will have 10 documents that live inside your Claude Project Knowledge folder. Claude writes all of them for you inside the chat — your job is to answer the questions, then copy and save each one as it's created.

Your 10 Project Documents
  • MASTER_PROFILE Your creative identity, what you are building, and why this project exists.
  • BRAND_VOICE How your writing should sound, what to use, and what to avoid.
  • VISUAL_STYLE Your aesthetic direction, colors, mood, photography style, and visual rules.
  • AI_TWIN_RULES How Claude should think, write, and support you specifically.
  • CONTENT_SYSTEM Your content pillars, hook style, caption style, CTA style, and first 30 ideas.
  • OFFERS_AND_IDEAS Offer ideas, positioning themes, and audience direction.
  • WORKFLOW_AND_TOOLS Your tools, how you work, and how Claude fits into your process.
  • NON_NEGOTIABLES The rules Claude should never ignore.
  • QUESTIONS_TO_REFINE What still needs clarity as your project grows.
  • FEEDBACK_LOOP The system for correcting Claude and making it smarter over time.
How to save each document into your Claude Project

All of this happens inside a regular Claude chat — no special tools, no file downloads, no extra apps. Once Claude writes a document, here is how to save it:

  1. 1Open your Claude Project and click Add content in the Knowledge section on the left sidebar.
  2. 2Select Add text content from the options that appear.
  3. 3Copy the full document Claude wrote in your chat and paste it into the text box.
  4. 4Title it using the document name shown above — for example, BRAND_VOICE — then save.
  5. 5Repeat for each document. One knowledge block per document.

Once saved, Claude will reference these documents automatically every time you work inside that Project. You never have to paste them again.

Who This Is For
You belong in Path One if any of these are true
  • You are brand new to AI.
  • You have opened ChatGPT once or twice but never trained it.
  • You do not have brand documents yet.
  • You do not know your exact voice yet.
  • You do not have clear content pillars.
  • You do not know what an AI Twin should even do.
  • You have messy ideas but no system.
  • You know what you like, but not how to explain it.

That is okay. This path walks you through building your Claude setup piece by piece.

The Flow

This is the full sequence you will follow inside Path One. Each step builds on the last.

  1. Start with the Blank Page Blueprint.
  2. Let Claude interview you.
  3. Answer messy.
  4. Let Claude pull out the patterns.
  5. Build the first working profile.
  6. Turn the profile into project documents.
  7. Test the voice.
  8. Test the visuals.
  9. Create the starter content system.
  10. Create the AI Twin rules.
  11. Use the feedback loop every time something feels off.
  12. Lock the baseline once it starts feeling aligned.

That is how someone brand new goes from "I have no AI history" to "Claude actually has something to work with." Because they are not transferring breadcrumbs. They are baking the first loaf.

Prompt One
The Blank Page Blueprint
When to use this

Use this first. This prompt helps Claude interview you and start building the foundation when there is no AI history to extract. Paste this into Claude before doing anything else.

↓ Copy & paste into Claude
I am setting up Claude for the first time, and I do not have previous ChatGPT history, AI history, saved prompts, AI twin context, brand documents, or a trained AI workspace to pull from. I am starting from zero. I want you to help me build a clear foundation for my creative identity, brand voice, content style, visual direction, AI twin, workflow, and project folder. Do not assume I already know how to explain myself clearly. Do not expect me to have polished brand language. Do not make this feel overwhelming. Do not ask me vague questions like "what is your brand voice?" without helping me understand what that means. I need you to guide me like a creative strategist who is helping me pull the useful pieces out of my own brain. Your job is to help me uncover: — who I am — what I create — what I care about — what my brand feels like — how I naturally communicate — what my content should sound like — what my visuals should look like — what kind of audience I want to reach — what tools I use or want to use — what I want AI to help me with — what should become part of my Claude Project folder Start by asking me detailed but simple questions, organized into these sections: 1. About Me — who I am, what I do, what season of life or business I am in, what I am building, and what makes my perspective different. 2. My Work, Brand, or Business — what I currently offer, what I want to offer, what I am known for, what problems I help solve, and what kind of people I want to attract. 3. My Voice and Communication Style — how I naturally talk, how I want my writing to feel, what kind of language I like and hate, with examples to help me choose. 4. My Content Style — what kind of content I want to create, where I want to post, what I want my audience to feel, and how I want my content to move people. 5. My Visual Style — colors, aesthetics, photography style, design style, mood, fonts, layouts, and what makes a visual feel like me. 6. My AI Twin — what I want my AI Twin to help me do, and what it should never do. 7. My Workflow and Tools — the tools I use or want to use, and how I like to work. 8. My Non-Negotiables — what I dislike, what makes content feel fake, what makes visuals feel wrong, and what I never want ignored. 9. My Project Folder Needs — what I want this Claude Project to help me with over time. After you ask the questions, wait for my answers. Do not build the profile yet. First, help me gather the raw material.

When to use this

Use this next, after Claude gives you the questions from Prompt 1. This sets the tone for how you answer — so Claude knows how to read between the lines of your messy, imperfect responses.

I am going to answer your setup questions, but I need you to help me think through them. I may not have perfect brand language yet. I may ramble. I may give messy answers. I may say things like "I do not know" or "kind of" or "I like this but not that." That is okay. Your job is to read between the lines and help me pull out the useful creative information. As I answer, I want you to look for: — patterns — preferences — tone and personality — values and visual taste — content themes and audience clues — offer ideas and communication style — things I care about and things I dislike — things that make my brand feel more like me Do not over-polish my answers. Do not turn me into a generic brand. Do not make me sound like everyone else online. Do not force me into a niche too quickly. Do not erase the messy details that may actually make the brand feel human. After I answer each section, I want you to do three things: 1. Reflect back what you heard — tell me what you understand in clear, simple language. 2. Pull out the useful brand context — identify what should be saved for my Claude Project folder. 3. Ask better follow-up questions — ask anything that would make the profile more specific, more accurate, or more useful. I want this to feel like we are peeling the layers back together. Not like a worksheet. Not like a corporate intake form. Not like homework. Start by asking me the first section only: About Me. Ask 8 to 12 thoughtful questions that help me explain who I am, what I care about, what I am building, and what makes my perspective different.

Create My Starter Content System
When to use this

Use this when you do not know what to post yet. This prompt builds a content system tailored to your profile — not random ideas, not generic pillars.

↓ Copy & paste into Claude
I am starting from zero and I need you to help me create a starter content system based on my working profile. I do not want random content ideas. I do not want generic pillars like educate, inspire, entertain, sell. I do not want you to give me content that could belong to anyone. I want you to build a content system that fits the creative identity, audience direction, voice, offers, interests, and goals we have uncovered so far. Create: 1. My Starter Content Pillars — 4 to 6 pillars that actually make sense for me. For each pillar: what it means, why it fits me, what my audience gets from it, example post topics, and what to avoid inside that pillar. 2. My Recurring Themes — themes I can come back to often, including personal stories, lessons, beliefs, client examples, behind-the-scenes moments, opinions, tutorials, mindset shifts, mistakes, transformations, or observations. 3. My Hook Style — explain how my hooks should sound, and give me 15 hook examples that fit my voice. Do not make them cliché. Do not make them recycled internet hooks. Do not make them too vague. 4. Give me 3 caption structure options I can reuse. 5. My CTA Style — explain how my calls to action should sound. Give me 15 CTA examples that fit my voice. Do not use overused CTAs unless you make them feel natural to my brand. 6. My First 30 Content Ideas — 30 ideas organized by pillar. Each idea should include: post topic, format, angle, why it fits my brand, and what the audience should feel or understand. 7. What We Still Need To Clarify — the parts of my content system that need more input before we lock anything in. Make this practical. Make it specific. Make it feel like the beginning of a real content engine — not a random idea dump.

Starter Feedback Loop for Beginners
When to use this

This is essential. Your Claude profile will not be perfect on the first pass — and it should not be. This prompt builds the system that lets you correct Claude and make it smarter over time.

↓ Copy & paste into Claude
I want to create a starter feedback loop for this Claude Project. Because I am starting from zero, I know the first version of my profile, voice, visuals, content system, and AI Twin rules will not be perfect. I want this project to get smarter every time I correct something. Create a document called FEEDBACK_LOOP.md with sections for: 1. Approved Outputs — examples that feel aligned. 2. Rejected Outputs — examples that felt wrong. 3. Voice Corrections — notes about tone, wording, rhythm, emotional depth, personality, and writing style. 4. Visual Corrections — notes about colors, lighting, aesthetics, image style, design, layout, and mood. 5. Formatting Corrections — notes about how I like responses structured. 6. Content Corrections — notes about captions, hooks, CTAs, content pillars, messaging, or audience fit. 7. AI Twin Behavior Corrections — notes about how I want Claude to respond, think, ask questions, make decisions, or support me. 8. Words and Phrases To Avoid — words, phrases, tones, or writing patterns I do not like. 9. Updated Rules Moving Forward — where we turn my corrections into rules. 10. Open Questions — things we still need to figure out. At the end, give me a reusable correction prompt I can paste anytime something feels off: --- Please add this to my feedback loop. What felt off: [EXPLAIN WHAT I DID NOT LIKE] What I want instead: [EXPLAIN WHAT WOULD FEEL BETTER] Where this applies: [VOICE / VISUALS / CONTENT / FORMATTING / AI TWIN BEHAVIOR / WORKFLOW / OTHER] Please update the relevant project rule so future outputs are more aligned. --- Make the correction prompt simple enough for a beginner to use.
