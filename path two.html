Path Two:
You Have AI History

Extracting the breadcrumbs. Bringing them into Claude.

This path is for the person who already has creative history sitting inside an AI tool somewhere.

Maybe they have used ChatGPT for captions. Maybe they have used Gemini for business ideas. Maybe they have used Claude for writing. Maybe they have used AI for branding, visuals, prompts, offers, content calendars, course ideas, social media posts, sales pages, image prompts, or creative direction.

That history matters.

Because even if it feels messy, random, scattered, or buried inside old chats, there are probably useful pieces inside it.

Their tone. Their preferences. Their corrections. Their formatting style. Their visual language. Their brand rules. Their "no, not like that" moments. Their repeated ideas. Their tools. Their projects. Their content patterns. Their creative fingerprints.

Path Two is about pulling that information out, cleaning it up, and bringing it into Claude so Claude does not feel like a brand new stranger with a keyboard.

The goal is not to copy another AI system into Claude forever. The goal is to give Claude a better first impression of who they are, how they create, and what their brand needs from the beginning.

What You Are Building

By the end of this path, you will have produced 9 core documents that live inside your Claude Project Knowledge. These become the creative operating system Claude pulls from every time you work inside that project.

The 9 Documents This Path Produces

MASTER_PROFILE Your core creative identity, brand context, audience, offers, and overall working profile
BRAND_VOICE Voice, tone, writing style, rhythm, phrases to avoid, caption and sales style
VISUAL_STYLE Aesthetic direction, colors, photography rules, AI image preferences, visual non-negotiables
AI_TWIN_RULES Identity-locking rules, prompt structure, visual consistency, styling preferences
CONTENT_SYSTEM Content pillars, formats, platform strategy, hook style, CTA style, recurring themes
OFFERS_AND_PROJECTS Offers, services, products, course ideas, brand ecosystem, clients, and active projects
WORKFLOW_AND_TOOLS Tools, how you use them, how you work with AI, how you give feedback
NON_NEGOTIABLES Rules Claude should never ignore, things you always correct, mistakes to actively avoid
FEEDBACK_LOOP A living document that captures corrections, approved examples, and updated rules over time
How To Save Each Document Into Your Claude Project
  1. Open your Claude Project and click Add content in the Knowledge section on the left sidebar.
  2. Select Add text content from the options that appear.
  3. Copy the full document Claude wrote in your chat and paste it into the text box.
  4. Title it using the document name shown above — for example, BRAND_VOICE — then save.
  5. Repeat for each document. One knowledge block per document.

Once saved, Claude will reference these documents automatically every time you work inside that Project. You never have to paste them again.

Who This Is For

Path Two is for people who already have AI history. They may have used ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Grok, or another AI assistant for any of the following:

Content Branding AI images Prompts Business ideas Offers Captions Strategy Research Visual direction Client work Course creation Sales pages Emails Digital products Workflow building

That means one of those tools may already hold pieces of their voice, preferences, creative patterns, brand logic, visual taste, and workflow. The job is to extract the useful parts of that history and bring them into Claude.

Before You Start

Before running the prompts, they need a place to save the results. They can use:

What you need ready
  • Google Docs, Notion, Apple Notes, Microsoft Word, or any plain text workspace where they can copy, paste, review, and organize information
  • Access to the AI account they have used the most for creative work — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, or another assistant
  • If they have multiple accounts, start with the one that has the most history connected to their content, branding, visuals, prompts, business projects, or creative direction

Once the AI gives them the extraction, they should save it. Not rely on keeping it buried inside a chat thread. This extraction is going to become part of their Claude setup.

Path Two Overview

For someone with AI history, they are not starting from zero. They are extracting what already exists. Here is the full flow from start to locked baseline:

  1. Run the Digital Breadcrumbs prompt in the AI tool they have used the most
  2. Save the extraction somewhere outside of the chat
  3. Run Peeling Back The Layers to go deeper
  4. Lightly clean up the extraction
  5. Bring the cleaned profile into Claude
  6. Ask Claude to build the working profile
  7. Make Claude prove it understands the profile
  8. Correct what feels off immediately
  9. Turn the profile into project folder documents
  10. Test the AI Twin with real outputs
  11. Create a feedback loop document
  12. Lock the baseline once the setup feels aligned

After Prompt 1

Once the AI gives the extraction, copy the full response into notes. Do not worry about making it perfect yet. The goal right now is to capture the raw material.

Do not delete too much too quickly. Sometimes the small details are the things that make the AI setup feel more like them later — a random formatting rule, a visual preference, a phrase they keep using, a way they describe their content, a correction they have given over and over again. That is the good stuff.

After Prompt 2

Once the deeper extraction is complete, save it with the first extraction. At this point they may have a lot of information — that is good. It may feel messy, repetitive, or include things that do not belong. That is normal.

The next step is not to make it pretty. The next step is to clean it lightly so it becomes useful.
